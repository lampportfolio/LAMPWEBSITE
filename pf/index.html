White Glove

Why Invest in 1st Trust Deeds?

First trust deeds are the senior-most lien position on a property, meaning your investment is first in line to be repaid in any scenario. With conservative loan-to-value ratios typically under 65%, there is a substantial equity cushion protecting your principal. These are the most secure form of real estate-secured lending.

Opportunities

Consistent monthly income backed by real property with short-term durations (typically 6–24 months). Senior lien priority means you are repaid first before any junior lien holders. Conservative LTVs create a significant equity buffer, reducing exposure to market fluctuations. Ideal for capital preservation strategies seeking attractive yields above traditional fixed income.

Risks to Consider

Real estate values can decline, potentially reducing the equity cushion. Borrower default may require foreclosure, which takes time and involves legal costs. Investments are illiquid during the loan term and are not FDIC insured. Returns depend on the borrower's ability to repay or refinance at maturity.

Why Invest in 2nd & 3rd Trust Deeds?

Junior trust deeds offer significantly higher returns in exchange for a subordinate lien position. LAMP PF mitigates this risk through rigorous CLTV analysis (typically under 65% combined), personal guarantees, co-invest team oversight, and borrower vetting. These investments are designed for investors seeking enhanced yield with disciplined risk management.

Opportunities

Higher investor returns (typically 10–13%+ annualized) compared to 1st TD positions. Short-term durations provide quicker capital return cycles. Personal guarantees add an additional layer of borrower accountability beyond the property itself. LAMP's co-invest team — including a foreclosure attorney and general contractor — provides hands-on deal oversight and rapid default remediation if needed.

Risks to Consider

Junior lien holders are repaid after the 1st trust deed holder in a liquidation event, meaning potential loss if property value declines significantly. Foreclosure on a junior lien is more complex and may require paying off senior liens. Higher default risk relative to 1st TDs. Investments are illiquid, not FDIC insured, and not guaranteed by any government agency. Past performance does not guarantee future results.
